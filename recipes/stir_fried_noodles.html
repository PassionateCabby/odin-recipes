Stir Fried Noodles

Description

Tsao mi fun are Taiwanese fried rice noodles. It's noodles that is stir fried with meat and vegetables.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 lb thinly sliced pork loin
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup rice wine
  • 1 tsp white pepper
  • 1 tsp Chinese five-spice powder
  • 1 tsp cornstarch
  • 4 dried Chinese black mushrooms
  • 1 (8oz) package dried rice vermicelli
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil, divided
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1/4 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp dried small shrimp
  • 3 carrots, cut into matchstick strips
  • 1/2 onion, chopped
  • 3 cups bean sprouts
  • 4 leaves napa cabbage, thinly sliced
  • salt to taste
  • 3 sprigs fresh cilantro for garnish

Steps

  1. Mix the pork with the soy sauce and rice wine. Sprinkle with white pepper, five-spice powder, and cornstarch. Set aside
  2. Soak the mushrooms in a bowl of cold water for 20 minutes, pour out the water, and cut off the stems. Slice the mushrooms thinly and reserve.
  3. Soak the rice vermicelli in a separate bowl of cold water for 10 minutes, pour off the water, and set aside the noodles.
  4. Heat 1 tbsp of vegetable oil in a wok over medium heat. Pour in the eggs and cook until firm and formed in a pancake. Remove pancake and slice thinly.
  5. Heat 2 tbsp of oil over high heat. Stir in garlic and dried shrimp and cook until the shrimp becomes aromatic after 20 seconds.
  6. Add pork with the marinade and cook for 4 minutes. Stir in the carrots and onion, and cook until soft after 3 minutes.
  7. Scrape pork mixture with the eggs and wipe the wok, turning it to medium-high heat.
  8. Heat remaining vegetable oil in the wok, stir in the drained rice vermicelli noodles. Cook and stir for a few minutes and add in the pork mixture.
  9. Scrape mixture into a serving bowl and garnish with cilantro to serve.
